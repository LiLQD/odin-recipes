Stir fry chicken with ginger

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 free-range chicken (about 800–900 grams)
  • 1 tablespoon of finely minced ginger and shallots
  • Fish sauce, salt, pepper, cooking oil
  • Red chili (optional)

Steps

  1. Clean the chicken, rub it with coarse salt and lemon to remove any odor, rinse well, pat dry, and cut into bite-sized pieces
  2. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, mix the chicken with 1 teaspoon salt, 1/2 tablespoon fish sauce, 1 tablespoon minced ginger, 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper, and 1/2 teaspoon seasoning powder.
    Let it marinate for 8–10 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  3. Heat the cooking oil in a pan. Add 1 tablespoon of minced shallots and 1/2 tablespoon of shredded ginger, and sauté until fragrant
  4. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and stir-fry over high heat. Stir well to ensure the chicken cooks evenly and doesn't stick to the pan
  5. When the chicken firms up and the sauce in the pan thickens and reduces, add another 1 tablespoon of fish sauce, 1 teaspoon of seasoning powder, 1/2 teaspoon of pepper,
    and chili if you prefer spicy food. Stir well to combine, then turn off the heat.
